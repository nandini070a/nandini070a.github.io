    Message from Anuradha Tuli, executive director
 

 

Do You Dream?

As yet another action packed year comes to a close, I sit and wonder what should my message dwell upon? In these exciting times there is so much to learn, so much to share and so much to dream about!

Do you dream? Well T.E. Lawrence in Seven Pillars of Wisdom said “All men dream:  but not equally.  Those who dream by night in the dusty recesses of their minds wake in the day to find that it was vanity:  but the dreamers of the day are dangerous men, for they may act their dream with open eyes, to make it possible.”

So hoping that he was right and all of you dream, I question - have you ever, in your busy and fast paced life, stopped to think about when you dream and what your dreams mean to you? I wonder - have you considered which one of the following dictionary meanings best define your dreams:

  1. An aspiration (wish, goal, hope, ambition, desire);
  2. A fantasy (pipe dream, castle in the sky);
  3. A vision (a nightmare, a hallucination, a delusion or a trance) or
  4. A delight (marvel, joy, pleasure, ide

How many of your dreams end up a vanity and how many do you make possible by having the courage of first recognizing that they exist and then recognizing that their achievement (in your lifetime or beyond) is not guaranteed? How far are you willing to go to achieve your aspirations ensuring that they are not left merely as castles in the sky? How much do you want from life such that your world is pleasurable and filled with joy devoid of nightmares and hallucinations! And if your dreams are castles in the sky how many are willing to do what Henry David Thoreau wrote in Walden “If you have built castles in the air, your work need not be lost; that is where they should be.  Now put the foundations under them.” 

Foundations on which dreams are nurtured into reality require the right attitude towards excellence, teamwork and leadership; all of these are also encapsulated in our PwC core values –

  1. Excellence achieved through innovation, learning and agility;
  2. Teamwork that is effective because it builds on strong relationships, respect and sharing with each other and
  3. Leadership which demands demonstrating courage, inspirational vision and unstinting personal integrity   

It’s a time ……to embrace excellence – to differentiate oneself from the quagmire of mediocrity that seems to have entrenched itself in all walks of life; to build teams and bridge the silos you have built around you; to envision and lead with impeccable courage and integrity.

It’s a time ……to tap the vast potential that exists in each one of you; to question “how can I do more?”; to say “I’ll find the time”; to think beyond short term failures; to get up stronger after every fall; to aspire for the impossible and make it possible. 

It’s a time …….to dream and to do; to let the air in your dreams and the fire in your belly kindle a flame that takes you on a journey where the sun never sets and every horizon is only a resting place before you embark towards the next.

I’ll end by quoting from Life's Little Instruction Book, compiled by H. Jackson Brown, Jr. “Don't say you don't have enough time.  You have exactly the same number of hours per day that were given to Helen Keller, Pasteur, Michaelangelo, Mother Teresa, Leonardo da Vinci, Thomas Jefferson, and Albert Einstein.”  And by adding, don’t say you don’t or can’t dream, I love to dream - we all do and must have the courage to recognize the same. 

Looking forward to the next year with the conviction that each one of us will dream with eyes open and not from the dusty recesses of our mind!!
