Deepak Mahurkar at 9th India Power Forum 2006
G. L. Tandon felicitating Deepak Mahurkar

PwC was invited to present a paper at 9th India Power Forum, hosted by India Energy Forum in New Delhi. Deepak Mahurkar, associate director, gave presentation on the ‘Internalizing Externalities’. In the session ‘Ensuring Fuel Supply – Coal and Gas’ chaired by G. L. Tandon, Ex-Chairman, Coal India Limited and NLC, Deepak Mahurkar presented views on externalities generated by fuels specially in power plants. 

After giving an overview of the external health and environmental effects of energy and transport sectors, Deepak explained the challenges in quantifying externalities. By illustrations it was explained that Fuel Cycles have variable externalities and that the technologies for reduction of impact need to be developed.    Deepak concluded with the view that the major element of new development model will be to decouple future economic prosperity from environmental pollution and even to make the economic-ecological relationship a positive instead of a negative one. The key of doing this will ultimately lie in the creation of a new clean technology base.

  Workforce Sustainability and Talent Management in the Indian Oil and Gas Upstream Industry

PwC’s Human Resource Services and Oil and Gas Practice knowledge partnered Petroleum Federation of India and concluded the study titled – ‘Workforce Sustainability and Talent Management in the Indian Oil and Gas Upstream Industry’. The study focuses on the human resource requirements and availability in the Upstream Industry and India’s potential to cater to domestic and global requirements. The study uses a demand-supply model to estimate manpower requirements for key skills over the next ten years. The projection model was developed in close consultation with industry experts and members of the academia using methods including structured interviews, expert discussions and surveys.

The study concludes with a section on suggested action steps for the Government, the Industry and the Educational Institutions to address the identified talent issues.

  The World in 2050: Implications of global growth for carbon emissions and climate change policy
PwC report ‘The World in 2050: Implications of global growth for carbon emissions and climate change policy’ by John Hawksworth, outlines a ‘Green Growth Plus’ strategy that could curb global carbon emissions without significantly reducing long-term economic growth.  As per the report, G7 economies will need to reduce their current level of emissions by around half by 2050 to achieve this ‘Green Growth Plus’ scenario, whereas the E7 economies would still be able to increase their emissions by around 30% from current levels. But this would vary considerably across the E7, with India able to more than double its emissions from current relatively low levels, while Russia would need to almost halve its emissions from current relatively high levels.
