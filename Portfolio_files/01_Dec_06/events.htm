Meet the new partner
Meet the new partner: Ashlesh Verma, executive director, Hyderabad

The journey began in 1991, when Ashlesh Verma joined the Audit practice of the after completing B.Com (Hons) and awaiting CA final results. It was his first job and he was promoted to Asst. Manager in 1993. Though he left the firm in 1994 for a three year period to work in the Industry, the turning milestone was in 1997 when he joined back to start the tax practice. In the early years, the Hyderabad practice had a small team - which was multi functional. “The challenge when we started the tax practice was the small team multitasking jobs coupled with changes on the regulatory front happening every day!”, recounts Ashlesh.

As he goes down the memory lane, he recalls that working with ITC's International business for three years was an enriching experience. “This is so far my career’s biggest achievements and now being the executive director, I would like to grow the practice and continue the leadership position in the local marketplace”, he adds.

As regards gadgets at work, he can’t work without his mobile and of course Lotus Notes, chuckles Ashlesh. He enjoys reading and was fond of fiction during college and article days. Of late this has been suffered a bit. In his words, “Currently I am reading Jeffrey Archer's False Impression and have simultaneously started reading an excellent book titled Your Legacy Leadership, which was presented to me at the recent A7 New partner’s event”.
Shilpa, his wife, is extremely happy with the work he does and even appreciates the long hours required to be put in. He smiles and says “Thankfully no work life balance issues!”.

Words of wisdom : 'People can manage themselves, if you let them!'
   'map' training rollout across India for Advisory

'map', a global initiative was introduced to advisory Project Managers across India by Aditya Sharma, associate director. A total of 12 two hour sessions were held in Kolkata, Delhi, Mumbai and Bangalore.  

'map' allows users to keep all of their relevant documents, project emails, and relevant details of the project, guidelines, and deliverable deadlines in a specified place. Documents that are present in other databases like Office 21, etc., can simply be linked to map instead of making a duplicate copy. However the entire map should always be replicated: one in the server and a local copy. All members view the same pages, thus increasing transparency.

   PwC Secondee
Abhay with his wife at Clearwater

Abhay Srivastava, TRS, Kolkata recounts his secondment in Florida January 2005.  It was the first time I was going to work outside India and was also my first visit to the United States.  When I reached Tampa, Florida,  I was dazzled by the beauty of the place and it didn’t take me much time to realise why Florida is called ‘The Sunshine State’. With pristine beaches and azure waters, it is one of the busiest tourist destinations in the country. The winters are especially pleasant here.

Both my wife and I love to drive, and driving on wide and immaculate interstates was a God gifted opportunity for us. We covered a big part of the Eastern US by road, visiting places like New York, Washington, Pittsburgh, Connecticut and Niagara Falls. We also traveled to the Great Smoky Mountains, with two wagons full of PwC friends. It was one memorable trip!

I am an Architect by graduation, and one of the most nostalgic time for me was when I visited the Master Architect Frank Lloyd Wright’s most famous creation, ‘The Falling Waters’ near Pittsburgh, in Pennsylvania.

The 15 months that I spent in Tampa not only accorded valuable professional experience to me, but also were immensely fun filled days.
